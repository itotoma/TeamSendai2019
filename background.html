Motivation

The mechanism that can be extended and contracted with both ends fixed to the structure is often used in nature. For example, muscles whose both ends are fixed to the tendon(fig. 1a), the network structure of Hydrodictyon which is a kind of algae(fig. 1b), and the contractile ring made of actin and myosin fixed to the cell membrane(fig. 1c). The advantage of these systems is that they can be resized while maintaining their structure. Currently, the size of DNA robots is determined permanently at the design phase, but by incorporating an actuator that maintains connectivity at both ends into a DNA robot, a more advanced DNA robot that can adjust the size by input will be realized.

fig.1 both ends fixed mechanisms are widely seen in nature

Merit

Venkataraman et al. (2007) reported a “polymerization actuator” that can be extended by HCR while maintaining both ends connectivity. The merit of this actuator is the almost continuous extension by HCR. In size control, taking various lengths has numerous advantages such as application to metamorphic robots. Therefore, in this project, by adding a continuous shrink function to the “polymerization actuator” that has continuous extend function, we try to create a useful actuator that can take various lengths reversibly.

Problem & Solution

Since the HCR autonomous reaction is driven by the difference in entropy between the hairpin and the main strand, it is not easy to shrink it. We evaluated the reaction of four-way branch migration that appears in the process of HCR energetically using the Nearest-Neighbor model and obtained the hypothesis that the before and after four-way branch migration is in a reversible state. Based on this hypothesis, we propose a method for reversing a stable extension reaction to a shrinking reaction by modifying the sequence and incorporating an irreversible process into the reaction.
